Facebook guidance platform

Senior Product Designer, Associate Project Manager

Helping sellers on Facebook commerce platforms navigate what to do next.

The Problem

Small and medium sellers on Facebook and Instagram IG don’t know what steps to take to grow their audience and sales.

The platform feels cryptic and complex, and growth is elusive.

Constraints

  • We don’t know what’s best, we don’t have the data/expertise
  • It’s expensive to guess how much of an impact guidance will have on the seller’s business

Divide and conquer

Guidance is broken into three main tiers based on urgency.

If your business isn't live because of an issue, you shouldn't see any guidance that isn't focused on getting things back online.

The anatomy of guidance

One-click experiences reduce friction, and they remain in the same flow users will find when they return to use the feature later to establish a familiar context

Users can dismiss irrelevant guidance and provide feedback about why they don’t want to see it.

Scaled, but tailored

Guidance is shown across many platforms and surfaces, like email, Shopify app, Facebook app

It's carefully tailored to sellers based on their jobs to be done and business stage.

Outcomes

  • Demonstrated the ability to drive seller behavior
  • Expanded seller access to guidance
  • More consistent seller messaging, less noisy communications for sellers in the first 7 days

What didn’t work

Guidance was developed too soon to see meaningful success. There was not enough data about what actually works, and not enough seller trust to get that data.
